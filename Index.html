> status: disconnecting...

A personal farewell  /  written by a teenager

I'm leaving
coding &
cybersecurity.

This is not failure.

This is not giving up.

This is change. This is growth.

scroll
01
Reflection

It started with
pure curiosity.

I was just a teenager.

No mentor. No roadmap.

Just a screen, a browser, and questions.

Late nights staring at terminals.

Tutorials half-finished at 2am.

Error messages I didn't understand.

But I kept going — because I wanted to know.

I started with curiosity.

Endless learning.

Trying to understand how things work.

02
The Struggle

No one told me
how lonely it gets.

Learning alone as a teenager is a different kind of hard.

No one to ask when you're stuck.

No one to celebrate when something finally works.

Just you, the silence, and the blinking cursor.

"Am I doing this right?
Does this even matter?"

I built things that broke.

Rebuilt them. Broke them again.

Shipped projects no one saw.

The effort was real.

The loneliness was real.

And through all of it — so was the growth.

03
Reality

Not every path is meant
to be permanent.

I used to think starting something

meant staying in it forever.

That walking away was weakness.

That changing direction meant I failed.

But that's not true.

Some chapters are meant to close.

Not because they failed us —

but because they finished.

They gave us everything they were supposed to.

And then it was time.

04
The Decision

I'm choosing
to step away.

Not because I can't.

But because I want something different.

This isn't defeat.

I'm not running from something.

I'm walking toward something else.

"That's a different kind of courage."

The terminal is still on my desktop.

The repositories are still public.

But the chapter is closed.

And closing it took more strength

than opening it ever did.

05
Closure

This chapter
mattered.

Every bug fixed at midnight.

Every concept learned in isolation.

Every moment of doubt pushed through.

It all shaped something in me.

Something that won't disappear

just because I've moved on.

Coding taught me how to think.

Cybersecurity taught me how to question.

Together — they gave me a sharper mind.

This chapter mattered.

It shaped me.

And I'm grateful for it.

end of chapter

This is not
the end.
Just a new beginning.

To anyone reading this —

who is also questioning,

also struggling,

also wondering if it's okay to change —

It is.

You are allowed to evolve.

You are allowed to choose differently.

That is not weakness.

That is the most human thing you can do.

Connect

— written with honesty, a little grief, and a lot of gratitude.
